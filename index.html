The Effects of Climate Change on U.S. Honey Bee Population

Title for Project: "The Effects of Climate Change on U.S. Bee Population"

Purpose of Project: The main purpose of this project is to highlight how climate change has contributed to the decline of Bees throughout the U.S. over the last 30 years. Through this visualization, people can better view how staggering the decline has been, the causes of the decline, and how the decline affects the health of our planet.

Story-Line: Bees may seem tiny and insignificant at first glance, but their role in in the health of our planet and our agricultural industry is substantial. These incredible insects are responsible in generating over $15 Billion a year annually for the U.S. producing many of our favorite crops such as apples, avocados, and blueberries, while also being involved in pollinating 90% of all wild plants. However, bees are now dying off at a rapid rate due to an increase in our planet’s temperature over the last 50 years. With predictions of even hotter weather to come, and increasing pressure from habitat loss and harmful parasites, it may not be long before we’ll have to struggle through a world without Bees.
