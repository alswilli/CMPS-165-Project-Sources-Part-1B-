The Effects of Climate Change on U.S. Honey Bee Population
Story-Line: Bees may seem tiny and insignificant at first glance, but their role in the health of our planet and our agricultural industry is substantial. These incredible insects are responsible in generating over $15 Billion a year annually for the U.S. producing many of our favorite crops such as apples, avocados, and blueberries, while also being involved in pollinating 90% of all wild plants. However, bees are now dying off at a rapid rate due to an increase in our planet’s temperature over the last 40 years and beyond. With predictions of even hotter weather to come, and increasing pressure from habitat loss and harmful parasites, it may not be long before we’ll have to struggle through a world without Bees. Through this visualization, we can better view how staggering the decline has been, the causes of the decline, and how the decline affects our daily lives.

Insights: There are quite a few insights that we hope people will gain from this visualization. One main insight is just how drastically the temperature has increased and the bee colony count has decreased over the last 40 years. This is shown to the viewer right upon opening the visualization within the line graph. This information is then further expanded with two U.S. data maps. Another big insight is the fact that bees are responsible for so many of the crops we consume every day. By looking at how the map and/or map tooltip visually shows the fruits that will be gone without bees pollinating them adds some importance to why their decline in numbers is a huge issue. A third insight viewers can take away from the visualization is seeing which states were mostly largely hit by the temperature increase and bee decline over the last 40 years. By visually seeing which states shared these characteristics, people can make connections as to why the decline was so heavy in those regions or why with some areas/temperatures bee colonies have continued to thrive.
